About Rubik's Cube

What is a rubik's cube? Where did it come from? Who invented it? Want to know more about the Rubik's Cube? You've arrived at the right place for the right information!

Invented in 1974 by Ernõ Rubik, a Hungarian architecture professor, the Rubik's Cube has always been the most beloved puzzle of children. Originally named as the "Magic Cube", Ernõ has also usedit as a learning exercise to illuminate his students about 3-dimensional spaces.

It was during the 1980s, when the Rubik's Cube started becoming a hype between people, mostly teenagers. Not only did it become a pop culture, Cubes were also featured in The Simpsons, The Big Bang Theory, and so many more to name...

Currently, the Rubik's Cube holds one of the top places in the world when it comes to mind exercises, or a healthy competition. Yes, it's the World Cube Association we're talking about. Since Feliks Zemdges broke 100 world records, cubing has become a spirit. The current world record to solve a 3x3 Rubik's Cube is 3.475s by Yusheng Du. That's quite amazing! What are you waiting for? Go set one for yourself!

New to Cubing? Don't worry, we've got you covered!

Officially founded by Ron van Bruchem of the Netherlands and Tyson Mao of the United States, the World Cube Association or the WCA has been a spark since 2004. Quite evident by their name, the WCA is an official cubing association and has hosted more than 7,500 competitions with more than 160,000 participants (as of June 2022) — numbers are expected to increase as more people gain knowledge about Rubik Cubes. The WCA hosts competitions all around the globe! For more info, you can check out more on their upcoming competition schedules.

Going by their name, the WCA is the world's biggest cubing association. From world records list to rules and regulations, they have it all. There's no harm in saying that it's the WCA because of which the cubing spirit is still alive. Every cuber has a dream of being recognized globally as a speedcuber, which wouldn't be possible without the WCA. Look up at Feliks Zemdegs, Max Park and Yushend Du. Well, if you believe it or not, the final achievement of every speedcuber is to be on the rankings of the WCA. So, what are you waiting for? Start practicing!
